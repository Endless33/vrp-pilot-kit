# VRP Pilot Operations Guide

## Purpose

This document provides high-level operational guidance for organizations participating in a VRP pilot.

It describes recommended operational practices before, during, and after pilot execution while preserving the confidentiality of the protected VRP runtime implementation.

The guide focuses on operational consistency rather than implementation details.

---

## Scope

This guide applies to:

- pilot preparation;
- deployment operations;
- validation activities;
- evidence handling;
- engineering review.

It does not apply to:

- protected runtime implementation;
- proprietary operational procedures;
- confidential engineering workflows.

---

## Operational Objectives

The operations guide aims to:

- support consistent pilot execution;
- encourage reproducible validation;
- preserve exported evidence;
- improve engineering traceability.

---

## Operational Principles

### Preparation

Participants should review the applicable public documentation before beginning deployment.

---

### Controlled Execution

Pilot activities should be executed in a controlled and documented manner.

Unexpected operational changes should be recorded.

---

### Evidence Preservation

Exported artifacts should be retained throughout the evaluation process.

Evidence should remain attributable to the corresponding pilot execution.

---

### Independent Validation

Validation activities should follow the documented public procedures.

Engineering conclusions should remain evidence-based.

---

## Recommended Operational Workflow

### Step 1 — Review Documentation

Review:

- deployment guidance;
- architecture overview;
- validation procedures;
- operational assumptions.

---

### Step 2 — Prepare Infrastructure

Verify that:

- infrastructure is ready;
- networking is configured;
- required resources are available.

---

### Step 3 — Execute Deployment

Deploy the pilot using documented public interfaces.

Record any operational observations.

---

### Step 4 — Execute Validation

Perform the planned validation activities.

Document any deviations from the intended procedure.

---

### Step 5 — Preserve Evidence

Collect and retain:

- deployment summaries;
- evidence bundles;
- validation reports;
- engineering observations.

---

### Step 6 — Perform Independent Verification

Review exported artifacts using documented validation procedures.

---

### Step 7 — Complete Engineering Review

Record:

- observations;
- identified issues;
- recommendations;
- final verdict.

---

## Operational Responsibilities

### Participant

Responsible for:

- infrastructure;
- deployment execution;
- evidence preservation;
- operational documentation.

---

### Validation Team

Responsible for:

- reviewing exported artifacts;
- documenting findings;
- supporting engineering assessment.

---

### Engineering Review

Responsible for producing the final documented technical conclusion.

---

## Security Boundary

The operations guide intentionally excludes:

- protected runtime implementation;
- proprietary engineering methods;
- confidential operational controls;
- internal runtime state.

---

## Operational Invariants

The operations guide preserves the following principles.

- deployment follows documented public procedures;
- engineering conclusions remain evidence-based;
- validation remains reproducible;
- protected implementation remains confidential;
- observable behavior remains the basis for operational assessment.

---

## Summary

The VRP Pilot Operations Guide provides a structured operational framework for planning, executing, validating, and documenting a public pilot while maintaining the separation between participant-controlled operations and the protected VRP runtime implementation.
