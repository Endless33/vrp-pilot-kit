# Pilot FAQ

## What is the VRP Pilot Program?

The VRP Pilot Program provides organizations with an opportunity to perform a structured engineering evaluation of VRP in an agreed environment before any production deployment.

The objective is technical validation rather than marketing demonstration.

---

## Is the Pilot free?

Pilot terms depend on the scope of evaluation, engineering effort, deployment complexity, and long-term collaboration objectives.

Each request is reviewed individually.

---

## Is this a public beta?

No.

Pilot participation is limited.

Every application undergoes technical review before acceptance.

---

## Does submitting an application guarantee acceptance?

No.

Submitting a Pilot application does not guarantee approval.

Pilot capacity is intentionally limited.

---

## Who should apply?

Typical Pilot participants include:

- Telecommunications companies
- Infrastructure providers
- Enterprise networking teams
- Critical infrastructure operators
- Systems integrators
- Research organizations

---

## Is the Protected Runtime included?

Only approved Pilot participants may receive authorized runtime components required for evaluation.

Protected Runtime implementation remains proprietary.

---

## Can we review the engineering documentation first?

Yes.

Public engineering documentation is available through:

- VRP Public Engineering Boundary
- VRP Validation Kit

Organizations are encouraged to review the public engineering materials before requesting a Pilot.

---

## Can we perform independent validation?

Yes.

Independent validation is an important engineering objective.

Public engineering artifacts are designed to support reproducible evaluation without exposing proprietary implementation.

---

## Will source code be provided?

The public repositories provide engineering documentation, contracts, schemas, SDKs, examples, and validation materials.

Protected Runtime implementation is not included.

---

## Can Pilot environments become production deployments?

Potentially.

Successful Pilot completion may lead to further engineering collaboration and production planning.

Production deployment is evaluated separately.

---

## How long does a Pilot typically last?

Pilot duration depends on:

- evaluation scope
- deployment complexity
- engineering objectives
- organizational requirements

The schedule is agreed individually.
