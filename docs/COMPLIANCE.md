# VRP Public Compliance

## Purpose

This document describes the public compliance model for the VRP pilot.

The objective is to define how pilot activities, documentation, exported evidence, and engineering procedures align with the published public documentation.

This document does not represent legal, regulatory, or certification guidance.

---

## Scope

This document applies to:

- public documentation;
- deployment guidance;
- validation procedures;
- evidence management;
- reporting practices;
- public integration contracts.

It does not apply to:

- regulatory certification;
- legal compliance;
- internal organizational policy;
- protected runtime implementation.

---

## Compliance Objectives

The public compliance model seeks to ensure that:

- published documentation is followed;
- validation procedures remain reproducible;
- engineering conclusions are evidence-based;
- exported artifacts remain attributable;
- public interfaces remain consistent.

---

## Compliance Principles

### Documentation Compliance

Pilot activities should follow the applicable public documentation.

---

### Evidence Compliance

Engineering conclusions should be supported by documented exported evidence.

---

### Process Compliance

Validation should follow the documented public workflow.

---

### Traceability

Artifacts, reports, and conclusions should remain traceable to documented pilot activities.

---

### Consistency

Equivalent pilot procedures should produce consistent engineering documentation.

---

## Compliance Areas

Typical compliance review includes:

- deployment documentation;
- validation execution;
- exported evidence;
- report structure;
- version identification;
- engineering observations.

---

## Compliance Assessment

A compliance review may verify that:

- documented procedures were followed;
- required artifacts are available;
- evidence integrity has been preserved;
- reports reference the correct public documentation version;
- conclusions are technically supported.

---

## Compliance Outcomes

Typical outcomes include:

### Compliant

Documented public procedures were followed.

---

### Partially Compliant

Minor deviations were identified but do not invalidate the engineering evaluation.

---

### Non-Compliant

Significant deviations prevent the documented validation process from being considered complete.

---

## Security Boundary

The public compliance model intentionally excludes:

- protected runtime implementation;
- proprietary engineering workflows;
- confidential operational procedures;
- internal security controls.

---

## Compliance Invariants

The public compliance model preserves the following principles.

- public documentation remains authoritative;
- engineering conclusions remain evidence-based;
- validation remains reproducible;
- protected implementation remains confidential;
- compliance review remains limited to public pilot materials.

---

## Summary

The VRP public compliance model provides a structured method for evaluating adherence to documented public pilot procedures while maintaining the separation between publicly observable engineering activities and the protected VRP runtime implementation.
